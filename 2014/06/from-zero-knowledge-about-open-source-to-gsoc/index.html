From Zero Knowledge About Open Source to GSoC

Tweet
03-06-2014
By Rachit Gupta

In Technology.

tags: gsoclibreofficefoss

It all started 2 years ago when we heard about a senior(Rishab Arora) doing his Google Summer of Code. At that time, I didn't know much about it. So, I started researching about it. Obviously I knew what open source softwares were, but never did I imagine the terminology will change the way I look at softwares!

I decided I will do my GSoC as well. I started with KDE, and chose Calligra as my project. I started working on a Junior Job. I successfully managed to push a patch! That was one of the best feelings I've ever had! Then came the time for GSoC, and down goes my laptop's processor! Dreams were shattered. Hopes were lost. I couldn't participate in 2013's GSoC.

But then, I realized it was not the end of life. I was fascinated by LibreOffice, with the fact they provide almost all the features of Microsoft Office with zero times the cost (something * 0 = 0). And it was Open Source! I decided to contribute to this fantastic software suite. I didn't have GSoC in mind then.

The sheer amount of code in the repository was too much for me. But, the community was really helpful, and I finally managed to solve my first Easy Hack after a lot of changes! I managed to solve 3 easy hacks in 2 months:

  1. fdo#52461
  2. fdo#74913
  3. fdo#74064

And then I decided to go for Google Summer of Code. I worked hard, faced some difficulties, but finally I managed to submit my proposal. And as you might have guessed, I got selected! :-D

If any of you wishes to know more about my work, keep watching this space! I'll be writing more about it soon. Feel free to drop an e-mail at rachitgupta1792@gmail.com.

Cheers!
